The Project

Why Air Quality Matters

Air pollution is one of the greatest environmental health risks of our time. The World Health Organisation estimates that over 7 million people die each year as a result of exposure to fine particles in polluted air.

AirIQ was built as a Data Science internship project to demonstrate how publicly available APIs can be harnessed to surface meaningful environmental insights through a clean, user-friendly web interface.

By combining Flask's server-side power, OpenWeatherMap's real-time API, and Bootstrap's responsive components, AirIQ provides instant, city-level air quality reports that are easy to understand and act upon.

Project Details
Academic Context

Submitted as part of a Data Science Internship programme. Demonstrates end-to-end full-stack development skills including API integration, backend logic, database management, and frontend design.

Open Source Stack

Built entirely with free and open-source tools: Python, Flask, SQLite, Bootstrap 5, and the free tier of the OpenWeatherMap API. No paid infrastructure required to run or deploy.

Data Privacy

Only a username, email, and hashed password are stored locally. No search history or personal location data is ever persisted. Passwords are hashed using Werkzeug's industry-standard PBKDF2 algorithm.

Data Source

All air quality readings come from the OpenWeatherMap Air Pollution API — a globally trusted meteorological data provider with coverage for thousands of cities.
